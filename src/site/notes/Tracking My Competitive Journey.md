When I first started competitive math, I treated it like a standard school subject—memorize the formula, solve the problem, and move on. That approach worked in a classroom, but it failed me the moment I stepped into high-intensity environments like the Berkeley mini Math Tournament (BmMT).

I realized that competitive math is not just about speed; it is about architectural thinking.

### The Shift in My Thinking

My approach has shifted from linear, step-by-step procedure to systemic analysis. I now view problems as structures where identifying the core logic is more important than the calculation itself. This requires a different kind of discipline—one that values the "why" behind a solution as much as the final answer.

### Why This Blog?

I am launching this space to publicly document my journey as a student in the competitive math community. This blog serves as my ledger for:

- **My Evolving Strategy:** I will be analyzing how I approach problems, what I learn from advanced study circles, and how I adapt to the pressure of tournament settings.
    
- **Resource Analysis:** I’ll be sharing the methods I find most effective, pulling from the insights I’ve gained while working with older students and peers who share this competitive drive.
    
- **Reflective Post-Mortems:** Success alone doesn’t teach you enough. I will be regularly analyzing my tournament performances to identify where I lost time and how I can improve my tactical decision-making for the next event.
    

This blog is a place for me to refine my logic, challenge my own assumptions, and share the evolution of my mathematical thinking.
